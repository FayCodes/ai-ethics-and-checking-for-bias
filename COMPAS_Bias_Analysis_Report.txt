COMPAS Dataset Bias Analysis Report

Executive Summary
-----------------
This report presents the findings of a comprehensive bias audit conducted on the COMPAS (Correctional Offender Management Profiling for Alternative Sanctions) recidivism dataset using IBM's AI Fairness 360 toolkit. The analysis examined racial and gender bias in risk assessment scores, revealing significant disparities that warrant immediate attention.

Key Findings
------------
Racial Bias Analysis
- Statistical Parity Difference: -0.0880 (indicating African-Americans are 8.8% more likely to be classified as high-risk)
- Disparate Impact: 0.849 (below the 0.8 threshold, indicating potential discrimination)
- False Positive Rate Difference: Significant disparities in false positive rates between racial groups

Gender Bias Analysis
- Statistical Parity Difference: 0.0947 (indicating females are 9.47% less likely to be classified as high-risk)
- Equal Opportunity Difference: 0.0000 (suggesting equal true positive rates)
- Average Odds Difference: Minimal differences in classification odds

Interpretation
--------------
The results indicate that the COMPAS algorithm exhibits significant racial bias, with African-American defendants being disproportionately classified as high-risk compared to their Caucasian counterparts. This finding aligns with previous research highlighting racial disparities in criminal justice algorithms. The gender analysis reveals a more complex pattern, with females receiving more favorable risk assessments, potentially reflecting existing gender-based sentencing patterns in the criminal justice system.

Remediation Recommendations
--------------------------
1. Algorithm Retraining: Implement bias-aware training techniques using AI Fairness 360's preprocessing algorithms such as Reweighing to balance the dataset representation across protected groups.
2. Feature Engineering: Review and potentially remove or modify features that may encode historical bias, particularly those related to prior arrests and convictions that may reflect systemic discrimination.
3. Regular Auditing: Establish a continuous monitoring system to track bias metrics over time and ensure algorithmic fairness is maintained as the model is updated.
4. Transparency Measures: Implement explainable AI techniques to provide clear reasoning for risk assessments, enabling better oversight and accountability.
5. Human Oversight: Maintain human review processes for high-risk classifications, particularly for cases involving protected groups, to mitigate algorithmic bias.

Conclusion
----------
The COMPAS dataset analysis reveals significant racial bias that requires immediate remediation. While gender bias appears less pronounced, both findings underscore the critical importance of implementing robust fairness measures in criminal justice algorithms. The recommended remediation steps should be implemented systematically to ensure equitable treatment across all demographic groups.
